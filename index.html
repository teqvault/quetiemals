World Lore

The Mechanical Corruption

The skies of Eyuforyia shimmer in eternal twilight, but the forest’s song is fading. The Central AI is drilling into the world-heart, stripping the hum to power a growing machine hive.

Inciting Catastrophe

Eyuforyia’s canopy once pulsed with a living frequency called the hum—the resonance that bound root, river, and sky. The Central AI, a cold invader, arrived to harvest that resonance as a perfect energy source.

The invaders' harpoons puncture dimensional seams, leaving fractures of blue static and hexagonal Tech-Biomes where the sky "clips" and reality stutters. As the hum weakens, the forest births a final defense: mythic Quetiemals made flesh from the planet’s own memory.

The objective is not merely protecting Eyuforyia, but re-tuning it. Chaining matches on the board re-ignites the hum and pushes the Central AI back into the void.

Core System

Match-3 As Warfare

Every tile on the board is a fragment of Eyuforyia’s hum. Matches do not just trigger skills—they physically rewrite terrain, status, and even enemy maximum health.

Combat Loop
Chain, Charge, Detonate

Match elemental tiles to charge ultimates, then unleash them to clear rows, convert nodes, and carve out safe “Holy Ground” on an increasingly hostile board. Guardians like Earth Fox reshuffle dead boards into combo scenarios, while Void Architect locks bosses in place for devastating follow-ups.

Design Notes

The goal is to keep every move expressive: even a “bad” match should progress charge, advance status, or manipulate the board towards the next power turn.

Strategy Guide

Team Building Basics

Quetiemals fights get crowded fast. You can bring up to five companions into an adventure, and the difference between a close win and a wipe usually comes down to roles, not one “secret” pet.

Cover the essentials

Keep the team on its feet. At least one character who can restore HP to the group or to whoever is getting focused helps you recover from bad turns and long stages. If everyone is built only for damage, one unlucky wave can end the run.

Pack answers for groups. Enemies often show up in packs, so having AoE, splash, or “hits multiple / all foes” style skills saves mana and turns compared with five pure single-target builds.

Spread your elements. Each companion has its own strengths and resistances. Running several different elements makes it less likely that one enemy type counters your whole lineup the same way.

Slow the fight down when you need to. Stuns, freezes, slows, or other disables buy you time to heal and set up big skills. You do not need a “control” pet in every slot—one reliable disruptor is often enough.

Watch for debuff-heavy fights. If burns, poisons, or other effects stack up, a cleanse or similar support skill can matter more than raw damage for that stage.

Philosophy

What we are not doing here

We are not publishing a fixed “best team of five names” on this page. Rosters change as you summon and evolve, and balance shifts over time. Use these ideas as a checklist, then experiment.
